Figma Workspace Reorganization

Timeline

2 weeks

Role

Design Operations

Tools

Figma, Notion

Organization

PLUS

Background

Problem


The design team at PLUS faced challenges in managing their growing number of Figma files. The existing system, which organized chronologically (e.g., Fall 2023, Spring 2022), made it difficult for new team members to find relevant files. This lack of organization led to inefficiencies, with team members struggling to locate past designs, and thus hindered collaboration and productivity.

How can the Figma workspace be restructured to enhance file accessibility and streamline collaboration for both designers and non-design team members?

Outcome


I created a streamlined and intuitive Figma workspace that uses a logical order based on product pillars and cross-functional teams, fostering a more productive and cohesive work environment.

End Result

Final Solution Showcase

Through the communication with the design lead and the whole design team, I created a Figma workspace that is organized in a logical order to ensure the files are easily discoverable and managable.

01. Teams

Within PLUS, we've grouped our teams into three main sections: PLUS - Universal, PLUS - Product Pillars (Training / Toolkit / Admin / Others), and PLUS - MISC. Each team is clearly separated from each other, making sure that PLUS members know where to find the files.

02. Universal Team

This is designers' go-to hub for a wide range of essential functions, such as Design Systems, Research Insights, Co-work Docs, and Marketing Work.

03. MISC Team

To make sure our non-designer colleagues have places to store their Figma files, we created PLUS - MISC, which includes four projects: Product Management, Research, Partnership & Tutoring, and Engineering.

04. Product Pillar Teams

Product Pillar teams are the main sections in PLUS app, including Training, Toolkit, Admin, and Others (Homepage, Login Portal, etc).

06. Files

In Product Pillar teams, each file should correspond to a Trello card and follows this naming convention: Product Feature / #Card Number / Designer First Name(s). There're file template and cover template available for creating new card files.

07. Moving Files

With our files set to be Trello cards, all design files are dynamic and can move between different project types based on the stage of the work.

Preparation

Research and Analysis

I began by studying Figma's tutorial on Team, project, and file organization and Spotify's Figma team structure for insights. Afterwards, I analyzed the app structure and existing Figma files to consider how to apply my insights to PLUS's team structure.

After the analysis, it seemed most intuitive to organize the Figma workspace by:
 • product pillars (main sections in app) + MISC (works not directly related to app)
 • product surfaces (sub-sections in app)
 • product features (features inside product surfaces)
 • design stages

But in what order?

Solutions

Prototyping

Using the five categories I identified in the analysis stage, I created four prototypes to experiment with different combinations of organizational structures.

After discussing with the design lead, we found that both designs faced challenges in seamlessly progressing through different stages of the design process. In Prototype A, designers had to manually transfer their designs from one page (e.g., WIP) to another (e.g., Review). Similarly, in Prototype C, designers were required to painstakingly copy the entire contents of a page from one file (e.g., WIP) and paste it into another file (e.g., Review).

Therefore, we decided to narrow down our prototypes to B and D. They were then presented to the entire design team, marking the final step toward a collective decision.

Collective Decision

Final Decision & Refinement

For the final discussion, I created the B and D prototypes in Figma to mirror the actual structure that designers will encounter when each prototype is actually implemented.

After a breif introduction of both prototypes and a voting process, prototype B wins!

Many designers found it intuitive to view a Trello card as a file and transfer it between various design stage projects. Also, since each file is a Trello card, designers will be able to have their personal creative spaces in the files and organize the pages as they want.

Designers also provided some feedback to help make the solution even better, such as specifying the definitions for design stages, creating a cross-functional team for developers / product managers / researchers, renaming MISC team to Universal team, etc. I also created a file template, cover template and slide deck for new Figma workspace onboarding.

Onboarding Guide

Reflection

This is my first time doing design operations work, which was so interesting. Although it wasn't a real "design" task, I used design thinking during the whole process, from analyzing common structures in other companies to creating prorotypes and asking for user feedback. After I compared the time used for finding a file in the original workspace and in the current workspace, I surprisingly found that the time spent decreased by 83%! It felt so good to witness how my work can make PLUS's workspace more organized and efficient for everyone.
